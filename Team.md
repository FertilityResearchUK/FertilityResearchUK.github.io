## Gwenda Burns

[Gwenda Burns.jpg](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1uueAsxjSdCeSxGAAgewPepB_TJSMbNzJ)

### Bio

Gwenda has spent more than 17 years in leadership roles supporting fertility patients across the UK, advocating for improved access to treatment and patient support. She has represented fertility patients across all four UK nations and in Europe, contributing to national and regional forums including the National Fertility Group, former CCG groups, government health working groups, the British Fertility Society, and Fertility Europe. Gwenda is a strong advocate for fair and equitable access to fertility treatment across the UK. Since 2010, she has been a member of Scotland’s National Fertility Group, which played a key role in achieving more equitable provision of services, including access to up to three cycles of treatment in line with NICE fertility guidelines. She remains committed to driving positive change for fertility patients and supporting research efforts that improve fertility care across the UK.

### Why are you involved in FRUK?

To help ensure that individuals facing fertility challenges are supported by evidence-based, forward-thinking solutions.

## Joris Veltman

### Bio

Joris Veltman is a Dutch human geneticist and Chair of Reproductive Genomics at the University of Edinburgh, where he also serves as Director of the Institute of Genetics and Cancer. His work has advanced understanding of the genetic causes of rare disease, and helped embed genomic approaches in clinical medicine. In 2016, he and Han Brunner received the King Faisal International Prize for Medicine. He was elected a Fellow of the UK Academy of Medical Sciences in 2021\. Outside work he enjoys running and spending time with family and friends.

### Why are you involved in FRUK?

I’m passionate about advancing fertility research, and I believe that setting up this charity will fill a critical funding gap—supporting high-impact studies and giving researchers the backing and opportunities they need to thrive in this vital biomedical field.

## Margot Wyrwoll

### Bio

### Margot Wyrwoll is a German clinician specialising in Clinical Genetics and Andrology. She works as a Senior Clinical Research Fellow at the Institute of Genetics and Cancer at the University of Edinburgh and as an honorary NHS Consultant in Clinical Genetics. She has identified several genetic causes of infertility and has translated these discoveries into clinical practice. Outside of work, she is a keen runner and loves spending time in nature.

### Why are you involved in FRUK?

I believe there is a clear unmet need, as no other charity in the UK is dedicated to supporting fertility research, putting the field at a significant disadvantage compared with other areas of medicine. By supporting FRUK, I hope to help strengthen the research environment and improve conditions for those working in this area.

## Anton Petrov

[Anton Petrov.png](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1_mwwoyo1I81MxFx7RIMQhL20tHpzKVN-)

### Bio

Anton Petrov is a bioinformatician specialising in RNA biology. He has led widely used international data resources, including RNAcentral and Rfam, and coordinated multidisciplinary teams to develop tools and shared infrastructure for RNA research. His work has centred on curating, organising and maintaining RNA data for the research community. He is also co-founder of an RNA biotechnology startup developing data-driven approaches to RNA discovery. Outside work, he enjoys rowing and playing guitar.

### Why are you involved in FRUK?

Having been personally affected by infertility, I feel strongly that advancing research is one of the most meaningful ways to improve outcomes, deepen understanding, and help reduce the impact of infertility for future families.
