  Joris Veltman is a Dutch human geneticist and Chair of Reproductive
  Genomics at the University of Edinburgh, where he also serves as Director
  of the Institute of Genetics and Cancer. His work has advanced
  understanding of the genetic causes of rare disease, and helped embed
  genomic approaches in clinical medicine. In 2016, he and Han Brunner
  received the King Faisal International Prize for Medicine. He was elected
  a Fellow of the UK Academy of Medical Sciences in 2021. Outside work he
  enjoys running and spending time with family and friends.
