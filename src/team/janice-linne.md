bio: >
  Janice Linne is a PhD student in the Adams lab at the University of
  Edinburgh and the Izsvák Lab at the Max Delbrück Center, investigating the
  genetic causes of infertility by studying chromosome segregation in early
  human embryos. Before she started her PhD, Janice worked for Novartis in
  Basel, Switzerland, and completed a Masters in Biomedicine at Karolinska
  Institute in Stockholm, Sweden. Her master's thesis was conducted at the
  Harvard Medical School studying endometrial cancer which sparked her
  interest in the overall topic of reproductive health, and ultimately drove
  her to pursue a PhD in fertility research. Outside of the lab, she enjoys
  playing tennis, running and reading.
quote_label: Why are you involved in FRUK?
quote: >
  I decided to volunteer with Fertility Research UK because I strongly believe
  that addressing the research gap surrounding infertility and advancing
  reproductive health requires not just scientific discovery but also
  systemic support for research and spreading awareness by bringing together
  researchers, clinicians, and people with lived experience.
