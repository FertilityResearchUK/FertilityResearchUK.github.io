But what you don't see are the many people and couples who go through ART without success—often facing repeated cycles, uncertainty, and emotional and financial strain.

Research can change that. Genetic and other biological markers may help predict which assisted reproductive approaches are most likely to succeed, supporting more personalised treatment and helping people avoid ineffective interventions.

**That's why Fertility Research UK exists:** to fund research that improves understanding, diagnosis, treatment and care—so more people can get answers, more treatments can work, and support can be better for everyone affected.
