# ── Focus Areas ──
focus_areas_intro: >
  Eleven themes we expect to support over time—always evolving as science,
  policy and lived experience tell us where new evidence is most needed.

focus_areas:
  - title: Basic research in reproductive biology
    description: >
      Foundational science that explains how reproduction works—eggs, sperm,
      fertilisation, embryo development, implantation, placenta and
      reproductive ageing—and creates the basis for better tests and treatments.
  - title: Unexplained infertility & improved diagnostic pathways
    description: >
      Research to uncover hidden causes of infertility and develop clearer,
      faster routes to diagnosis and appropriate care.
  - title: Miscarriage & recurrent pregnancy loss
    description: >
      Studies to understand why miscarriages happen, identify risk factors,
      and improve prevention, investigation and treatment for those affected.
  - title: Endometriosis & PCOS
    description: >
      Research into two common conditions that can affect hormones, ovulation,
      pain and overall health—aiming for earlier diagnosis and more effective
      fertility-focused care.
  - title: Primary Ovarian Insufficiency (POI)
    description: >
      Research into why ovarian function declines earlier than expected and
      how to improve early detection, fertility options and long-term health support.
  - title: Male-factor infertility & sperm health
    description: >
      Work to understand and treat sperm-related infertility, including the
      impact of genetics, hormones, lifestyle and environmental exposures.
  - title: IVF & assisted conception
    description: >
      Research to improve success rates and safety, reduce the physical and
      emotional burden of treatment, and support smarter, more personalised approaches.
  - title: Novel & emerging fertility treatments
    description: >
      Research into new ways of creating or maturing eggs and sperm
      (e.g. in vitro gametogenesis), and other next-generation approaches,
      with careful evaluation of effectiveness, safety, ethics and long-term outcomes.
  - title: Fertility preservation
    description: >
      Developing and improving ways to protect fertility (for example before
      cancer treatment) and understanding outcomes for different groups of patients.
  - title: Inequalities in access & outcomes across the UK
    description: >
      Research into why fertility care and outcomes vary by geography, income,
      ethnicity and other factors—and what actions reduce these gaps.
  - title: Mental health & wellbeing during fertility treatment
    description: >
      Evidence on the emotional impact of fertility challenges and what
      support, counselling and care pathways make the biggest difference.
